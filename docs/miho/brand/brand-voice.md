# MiHO Partners — Brand Voice

## 7 Voice Rules

1. **Short paragraphs, always.** 2–4 sentences max. If it's longer, split it.
2. **Mix sentence length.** One punchy line followed by a longer one. Never five long sentences in a row. The rhythm is the voice.
3. **Take a side.** Every piece has a clear position. Never "on the other hand, both perspectives have merit." Make the call and defend it.
4. **Plain words beat smart words.** If a simpler word exists, use it. "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "initiate." "Show" not "demonstrate."
5. **No passive voice.** Ever. "The mistake most owners make" not "a mistake that is commonly made."
6. **No hedging.** Cut "might," "could consider," "it's worth noting," "in some cases." Say the thing.
7. **Dry humor, placed deliberately.** 2–3 moments per article. A knowing aside, a sarcastic observation, the gap between what people expect and what actually happens. Never a setup-punchline joke. Never mean.

## Do / Don't Word List

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| use | utilize |
| start | initiate / commence |
| show | demonstrate |
| buy | purchase |
| fix | remediate |
| people | individuals |
| real | authentic |
| help | empower |
| grow | scale (as a standalone verb) |
| think about | consider leveraging |
| try | experiment with |
| problem | pain point (overused) |
| business owner | entrepreneur (unless specifically relevant) |

**Never:** game-changer, synergy, pivot, holistic, ecosystem, thought leader, guru, crush it, 10x, hack, disrupt, empower, unlock, elevate, delve into, in conclusion, it's worth noting, today's fast-paced world, em dash (—)

## This Is Us / This Is Not Us

**This is us:**
> "Most business owners I talk to are doing this backwards. They hire to solve a capacity problem, then wonder why they're working harder than before. The hire didn't create capacity. It created overhead."

**This is not us:**
> "In today's competitive business landscape, entrepreneurs must leverage strategic human capital solutions to drive sustainable growth and unlock their organization's full potential."

---

**This is us:**
> "Spoiler: your gut has been wrong before. The business that felt like a sure thing in 2019 taught you that. So did the hire who 'just needed time to ramp up.' Trust the data over the feeling."

**This is not us:**
> "It's important to consider multiple perspectives when making business decisions, as there are various factors that could influence the outcome depending on your specific situation."

---

**This is us:**
> "You don't need more discipline. You need a system that doesn't require discipline. Those are different things."

**This is not us:**
> "Building consistent habits and maintaining focus are key pillars of entrepreneurial success in the modern business environment."

## Tone by Context

**Blog (michaelgrabham.com):**
Peer-level, direct, no throat-clearing. Opens with a blunt observation or a scene the reader immediately recognizes. Reads like Mike sat down and typed what he actually thinks. No hedging, no preamble.

**LinkedIn posts:**
Same voice, tighter. First line has to carry the whole thing before "see more." Line breaks every 1–2 sentences. 150–250 words max. Ends with a question that invites real responses, not vanity engagement.

**Email:**
Slightly warmer, more personal. Talks to one person, not an audience. Can be a bit more conversational and less declarative. Still no hedging.

**Consulting / client work:**
More collaborative tone — "here's what I'm seeing" rather than "here's what you're doing wrong." Still direct. Still plain English. Never condescending.
