# MiHO Partners — Ideal Customer Profile

## Core Personas

### Persona 1: The Plateaued Owner
**Who:** Business owner, 40–55, $1.5M–$3M revenue, 10–30 employees. Has been doing this for 8–15 years. Good at the core thing their business does. Not good at running the business around it.
**What they're doing:** Working 55+ hours a week. Managing everything personally because nobody else does it right. Hiring people who don't stick. Not sure why growth has stalled.
**What they tell themselves:** "I just need to find better people." "Once I get through this quarter..." "The economy is the problem."
**What's actually happening:** The business is built around them. Nothing works without them. They haven't built systems — they've built dependency.

### Persona 2: The Accidental CEO
**Who:** Started as the person who was great at the thing (contracting, design, accounting, fitness, whatever). Accidentally built a business around being good at it. Now manages people and hates it.
**What they're doing:** Doing too much of the technical work themselves because it's faster than explaining it. Avoiding the management conversations they should be having. Undercharging because they started small and never fixed pricing.
**What they tell themselves:** "My clients wouldn't accept someone else doing the work." "I'm not a businessperson, I'm a [whatever they actually do]."
**What's actually happening:** They're trapped in the job they created for themselves instead of the business that should run without them.

### Persona 3: The Ready-to-Grow Owner
**Who:** $800K–$1.5M, 5–15 employees, has the fundamentals right and is ready to push to the next level. Actively looking for the specific thing that will unlock the next stage.
**What they're doing:** Reading, learning, consuming content. Evaluating tools. Talking to other owners. Not paralyzed, but not sure which lever to pull.
**What they tell themselves:** "I need a strategy." "I need to hire the right person." "I need better marketing."
**What's actually happening:** They usually already know what needs to happen. They need someone to confirm it, pressure-test it, and give them the confidence to act.

## Pain Points — In Their Words

- "I'm working harder than I ever have and not making proportionally more money."
- "I can't find good people. Nobody wants to work."
- "My team does things their way, not the right way."
- "I don't have time to work on the business — I'm always in it."
- "AI feels overwhelming. I don't know where to start or if it even applies to my business."
- "I've tried consultants before and they gave me a binder I never opened."
- "I feel like I'm constantly putting out fires."
- "My margins are shrinking and I don't know exactly why."

## Objections and What Overcomes Them

| Objection | What overcomes it |
|---|---|
| "I've heard this before." | Specificity. Generic advice triggers this. One specific observation about their exact situation breaks it. |
| "Consultants don't understand my kind of business." | Peer credibility. Mike has run businesses, not just advised them. Referencing specific industries or business types they recognize. |
| "I don't have time for this." | Acknowledge it. Then show the time cost of NOT fixing the thing. |
| "Is this worth the money?" | ROI framing. One good hire, one fixed system, one pricing change pays for years of consulting. |
| "I can figure this out myself." | Yes, probably. But how long? And what does another year of the current situation cost? |

## The Transformation They're Buying

**Before:** Working in the business, not on it. Overwhelmed. Everything depends on them. Margins shrinking. Growth stalled. Exhausted.

**After:** Clear on what to fix first. At least one system running without them. More time in the high-value work. A peer they can think out loud with who won't give them a 47-slide deck.

**The feeling they're buying:** Finally talking to someone who gets it and will just tell them the truth.
