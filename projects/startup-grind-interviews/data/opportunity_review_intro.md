# Opportunity Signals from Startup Grind Seattle Interviews

**124 nuggets** pulled from 17 Startup Grind Seattle interviews (founders, VCs, and
operators, 2014-2018; 17 of a planned 50). Each is an "opportunity signal" — a pain
point or piece of insider knowledge that reveals something a founder could actually
build or solve for. Fundraising/deal-structure content (VC pitch mechanics, cap tables,
equity/debt) is excluded even when the rest of the nugget is on-topic.

Each nugget is tagged on two axes:

**Topic** — the business function it's about: customer discovery, product/MVP
strategy, growth & marketing, hiring & people ops, pricing & unit economics, ops &
failure modes, leadership & founder psychology, market timing & competitive dynamics.

**Type** — not a quality rank, three different kinds of usefulness:
- **A, live gap** — names a specific unsolved pain point or market gap
- **B, replicable practice** — a tactic another founder could copy
- **C, color/anecdote** — instructive, not directly actionable

### The three types, same topic (Growth & Marketing)

- **A** — Rand Fishkin: 49% of Google searches now end with zero clicks — rank-tracking
  tools get *more* valuable as raw traffic disappears, since tracking rank becomes the
  only way to measure performance at all.
- **B** — Rand Fishkin: SparkToro gates its free tools behind a Twitter OAuth connect
  instead of an email address — cheaper API access and proof of its "followers ≠
  influence" thesis in one move.
- **C** — Marc Barros: Moment's two Kickstarter campaigns raised ~$1M combined — a
  reminder that a second campaign's success is genuinely uncertain, not a given after a
  strong first one.

### One example per topic

- **Customer discovery** — Rand Fishkin: keyword research for Crowd Cow found ~zero
  search volume for "buy steak online" — beef reads as a commodity, not a connoisseur
  category, so SEO couldn't work; had to find where the audience already was.
- **Product/MVP strategy** — Enrique Godreau III: when a company's telecom market died,
  it pivoted the same fault-tolerant tech into defense and media — industries needing
  the same underlying capability.
- **Growth & marketing** — Marc Barros: GoPro beat Contour partly because it was worn
  visibly on a helmet — every user became a walking ad; Contour's design was invisible.
- **Hiring & people ops** — Marc Barros: Moment never hires off the street — every hire
  starts as a paid side project first, which reveals more in a day than any interview.
- **Pricing & unit economics** — Marc Barros: big-box retail (Best Buy, Apple) is a
  hidden cash trap — net-90 payment terms vs. net-60 supplier terms, and banks only
  lend against 50% of inventory value.
- **Ops & failure modes** — Enrique Godreau III: a company beat sales forecasts for two
  years, then cratered — sales had been "icing" renewals of customers who'd already
  stopped using the product.
- **Leadership & founder psychology** — Marc Barros: his core failure as first-time CEO
  was never shifting from "founder" (do it all yourself) to "CEO" (build systems).
- **Market timing** — Enrique Godreau III: backed an Uber-concept taxi startup 13 years
  early. His own words at Uber's $400M raise: "rats, 13 years too early."
